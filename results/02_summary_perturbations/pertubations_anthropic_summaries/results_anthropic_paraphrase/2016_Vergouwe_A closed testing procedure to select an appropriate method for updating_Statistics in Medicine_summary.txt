This research introduces a structured, closed testing approach designed to systematically identify the optimal strategy for updating clinical prediction models when they are deployed in new patient groups. The paper outlines three distinct update techniques, ordered by their increasing complexity: recalibration in the large (which adjusts only the intercept), recalibration (which modifies both the intercept and slope), and model revision (which involves re-estimating all model coefficients). The proposed closed testing procedure employs a sequence of likelihood ratio tests, carefully controlling the overall Type I error rate. This method guards against overfitting when dealing with limited data by favoring less complex updates unless there is robust evidence warranting a more intricate adjustment. The efficacy of this procedure is illustrated through three clinical case studies involving prostate cancer screening, traumatic brain injury, and pediatric fever, showing that it favors simpler methods in smaller datasets and avoids overfitting, a contrast to automatic model revision. Furthermore, simulation experiments validate that the procedure effectively preserves acceptable Type I error rates (ranging from slightly above 0.05 in smaller samples to converging around 0.05 with larger datasets) and results in reduced mean squared error for coefficient estimation when compared to a comprehensive model revision process.
