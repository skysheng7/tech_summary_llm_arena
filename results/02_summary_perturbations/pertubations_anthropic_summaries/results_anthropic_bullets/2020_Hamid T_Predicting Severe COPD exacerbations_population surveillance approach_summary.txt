*   **Study Objective**: Researchers developed and validated prediction algorithms using British Columbia's administrative health databases (1997-2016) to identify COPD patients at high risk of severe exacerbations requiring hospitalization within 2 months.
*   **Methods**:
    *   The study compared multiple prediction approaches (logistic regression, random forest, neural network, and gradient boosting).
    *   6 months of health data were used to predict hospitalizations.
    *   Separate development (108,433 patients) and validation (113,786 patients) datasets were used.
*   **Key Results**:
    *   The gradient boosting algorithm achieved the best performance.
    *   Its AUC was 0.82 (95% CI: 0.80-0.83).
    *   This significantly outperformed the current standard of care based solely on exacerbation history (AUC: 0.68).
*   **Clinical Utility**:
    *   The model demonstrated practical value for population surveillance.
    *   For example, flagging 10,000 high-risk individuals would identify 539 patients who will actually experience severe exacerbations.
    *   This corresponds to a 5.4% yield and a number-needed-to-contact of 19.
*   **Implications**:
    *   This proof-of-concept study demonstrates that routinely collected administrative data can accurately predict imminent COPD hospitalizations.
    *   This potentially enables health systems to proactively target high-risk patients for preventive interventions.
