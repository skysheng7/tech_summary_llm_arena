Among these diverse NLP applications, its utility in the specific domain of identifying and flagging deceptive or fabricated news content, commonly referred to as fake news detection, is highlighted. In order to effectively address the challenge of fake news detection, the researchers put forth and advocate for a multimodal methodology. This proposed approach aims to achieve a more comprehensive and robust detection by synergistically integrating both textual information, derived from the content of the news, and visual elements, such as images or graphics associated with the news. The implementation of this multimodal strategy relies upon the skillful deployment and combination of several transformer-based models, which are known for their advanced capabilities in processing sequential data. The underlying architectural design of the DeBERTNeXT system, as proposed by the authors, is structured around three principal and interconnected segments. Firstly, there is a dedicated textual input module, responsible for processing and understanding the language component of the news. Secondly, a complementary visual input module is in place, designed to analyze and interpret the visual aspects of the news. Finally, the crucial fusion module serves the purpose of amalgamating, or merging, the distinct outputs generated by both the textual and visual input modules, thereby creating a unified representation. The efficacy and superiority of this comprehensively designed approach are further substantiated by the empirical findings derived from extensive experimentation, which conclusively demonstrate that the proposed method significantly surpasses the performance benchmarks set by existing state-of-the-art techniques when evaluated on the Fakeddit dataset, a recognized benchmark for fake news detection research.
