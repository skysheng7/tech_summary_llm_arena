*   Proposes a *closed testing procedure* to determine the extent of updates for an existing logistic-regression prediction model when it performs poorly in a new population.
*   Considers a hierarchy of updating options with increasing complexity:
    *   Keep the original model.
    *   Update only the intercept (recalibration-in-the-large).
    *   Update intercept plus a global slope (recalibration/logistic calibration).
    *   Re-estimate all coefficients (model revision).
*   The method uses a sequence of likelihood ratio tests.
*   It starts with the most complex model (revision).
*   If justified by the data, it moves toward selecting simpler updates.
*   This approach limits unnecessary overfitting.
*   It approximately controls the overall Type I error.
*   In three clinical case studies, the procedure often selected parsimonious updates.
