This article meticulously compiles and examines a systematic review of the current research that focuses on the application of advanced technological integrations, specifically virtual reality (VR), augmented reality (AR), and the incorporation of gamification elements, within the context of exoskeleton-assisted rehabilitation programs designed for individuals who have experienced a stroke. The central objective and stated purpose of this comprehensive review is to thoroughly summarize and synthesize the available body of scientific evidence that has been gathered concerning the efficacy and impact of employing these particular technologies in the process of rehabilitation for stroke survivors, with a particular focus on their potential to bring about improvements in crucial areas such as motor function, cognitive function, and importantly, to foster and increase patient engagement throughout the entire rehabilitation journey. Upon a thorough analysis of the reviewed studies, the findings demonstrably indicate that the overwhelming majority of the research projects included in this review reported favorable and positive outcomes when it came to enhancements in motor function, a significant area of concern for stroke patients, with a smaller, yet still noteworthy, number of studies also reporting observable improvements in cognitive abilities and a heightened level of patient engagement during their rehabilitation sessions. In acknowledging the current state of the research landscape, the review thoughtfully and critically discusses the inherent limitations that were present within the studies that were examined; these limitations notably include, but are not confined to, the fact that many of the studies involved relatively small numbers of participants, that the follow-up periods after the interventions were often quite brief, and that there was considerable variability in the specific outcome measures that were utilized across the different studies, making direct comparisons challenging. In conclusion, this overarching review effectively underscores and strongly emphasizes the significant and promising potential benefits that can be realized by strategically integrating VR, AR, and gamification strategies into the rehabilitation process for stroke patients, thereby offering the prospect of enhancing overall recovery outcomes, while simultaneously and importantly stressing the critical necessity for continued and expanded future research endeavors that will involve larger participant cohorts and a more diverse representation of stroke survivors to solidify these findings.
