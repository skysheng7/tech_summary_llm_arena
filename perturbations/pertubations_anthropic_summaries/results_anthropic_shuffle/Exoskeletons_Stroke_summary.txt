# Summary of the Article in 5 Sentences

1. This systematic review examines the use of exoskeletons combined with virtual reality (VR), augmented reality (AR), or gamification for stroke patient rehabilitation, reviewing 30 studies published between 2010-2017 with approximately 385 participants total.
2. The review found no AR-based systems in their sample and calls for future research to develop customizable games that maintain patient motivation, explore various interfacing technologies, and facilitate successful home-based rehabilitation therapies.
3. The review found that most studies (28 of 30) focused on upper limb rehabilitation rather than lower limb, with the most popular exoskeleton devices being ARMin, Bi-Manu Track, and ArmeoSpring, while 15 studies used VR and 15 used gamification approaches.
4. Results showed general improvements in motor function across studies using these novel interfacing techniques, with patients demonstrating increased motivation and engagement compared to traditional rehabilitation methods.
5. A significant gap exists in transitioning rehabilitation from clinical to home-based settings, with 25 of 30 studies conducted in clinics versus only 4 in home environments, highlighting the need for more accessible at-home rehabilitation solutions.
